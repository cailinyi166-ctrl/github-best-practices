## 2. Discovery: High-Signal Filtering üì°

- [cite_start]**Explore Tab**: Use the "Explore" feature (via the top-left menu) to find projects tailored to your tech stack and environment[cite: 10].
- **HelloGitHub Strategy**: To avoid information overload, use [HelloGitHub](https://hellogithub.com) as a filter. [cite_start]Review their monthly/weekly reports for curated high-quality projects, then visit the official GitHub repo for deep dives[cite: 10].

## 3. The Decision Tree: Is it worth your time? üå≥

[cite_start]Before deep-diving into a recommended English project, ask yourself these three questions[cite: 12, 13]:

| Question | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- |
| **New Framework?** | [cite_start]Does it offer a logic/methodology that is significantly faster (e.g., 10x) than your current one? [cite: 14] |
| **Productivity Boost?** | [cite_start]Can it directly automate your tasks (e.g., auto-generating weekly reports)? [cite: 15] |
| **Code Portability?** | [cite_start]Can you "migrate" or reuse specific functions in your own projects? [cite: 16] |

[cite_start]**Action**: If you answer **YES** to at least one, it is worth exploring the source code[cite: 17].

## 4. Advanced Search Hacks üîç

[cite_start]Precision searching saves your most valuable resource: time[cite: 19].

- [cite_start]**Scoped Search**: Use `in:name` or `in:description` followed by keywords (e.g., `in:name automation`) to find repositories with specific terms in their title or summary[cite: 20].
- [cite_start]**The "Awesome" Prefix**: Prefix your search with `awesome` to find curated lists of high-quality resources and projects managed by experts[cite: 21].

## 5. Philosophy of Reading READMEs üí°

- [cite_start]**AI Collaboration**: If a README is too complex, use AI to summarize the logic and usage[cite: 22, 23].
- **Focus on the "Why"**: Don't just look at *how* to use it. [cite_start]Always ask: *"Why did the creator start this? What specific problem or framework were they trying to build?"* [cite: 24]
